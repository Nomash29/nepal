Explore Nepal

From the highest mountains to rich cultural heritage

Geography

Nepal is a landlocked country in South Asia, bordered by China and India. It features diverse landscapes, from the flat plains of Terai to the world’s tallest peak, Mount Everest. The Himalayan region attracts trekkers and adventure seekers from around the globe.

Culture & Languages

Nepal’s culture is a blend of Hinduism, Buddhism, and various ethnic traditions. The official language is Nepali, but over 120 local languages are spoken. Nepal is known for its art, architecture, music, and age-old customs.

Traditional Food

  • Dal Bhat: Lentils with rice and vegetables, eaten daily by many Nepalese.
  • Momo: Steamed or fried dumplings filled with vegetables or meat.
  • Sel Roti: A sweet, ring-shaped rice bread, often made during festivals.
  • Gundruk: Fermented leafy green vegetable dish.

Festivals

  • Dashain: The longest and most significant Hindu festival in Nepal, symbolizing the victory of good over evil.
  • Tihar: Known as the festival of lights, similar to Diwali in India, honoring animals and gods.
  • Holi: The colorful spring festival where people throw colors and celebrate joyfully in the streets.
